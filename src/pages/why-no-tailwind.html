Why I don't use Tailwind on SaturnCI

I don't use Tailwind on SaturnCI. I have used Tailwind before. This is not ignorance. It is a choice.

The cost of dependencies

Every dependency is a decision. You adopt it. Then you maintain it. Then you upgrade it. Or you don't upgrade it, and it becomes a weight.

[More here about the ball-and-chain effect]

The Bootstrap graveyard

I have done the Bootstrap to Tailwind migration. It was not pleasant.

[Details of the migration: what broke, how long it took, the pain involved]

What I use instead

I use my own utility class library. It does what I need. I control it.

When Tailwind makes sense

Tailwind is good for some things. Prototypes. Throwaway projects. Work with short time horizons.

If you are building something you will abandon in two years, use Tailwind. It will help you move fast.

Why SaturnCI is different

I intend to maintain SaturnCI for decades. A different time horizon requires a different dependency calculus.
