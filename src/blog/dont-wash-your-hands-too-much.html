Don't wash your hands too much

The idea that choosing not to practice TDD some indeterminate amount of time, under certain unspecified conditions, is a cope.

For people who write tests sometimes and not other times, the truth is usually that writing tests takes them a long time because they're not very good at it. Which is fine! There's no shame in that. No one is born knowing how to do everything. You have to crawl before you can run.

I'll even admit that it's true that shooting for 100% test coverage is probably not a wise policy for someone who's not very practiced in writing tests. You'd probably spend 95% of your time learning and struggling and only 5% of your time actually creating value. I personally didn't start my career writing tests for everything. I had to work up to it.

Programmers who know how to write tests also sometimes skip them out of sheer impatience. I myself do this sometimes.

Another reason for skipping tests is pure and simple laziness. Again, I'm occasionally guilty of this myself!

I only ask this: let's not get mixed up, and let's not bullshit ourselves. When I choose to be lazy or impatient, I admit it. It's not the biggest deal in the world. I got lazy today, so what. I'll try to do better tomorrow.

When I skip steps because I'm learning, it's because I'm learning! It's not because I know how to do everything yet I'm choosing to skip steps sometimes to be "pragmatic".

If you don't practice TDD 100% of the time, or if you don't have 100% test coverage, I hope you don't feel guilty about it. But don't let anyone trick you into thinking that choosing not to write tests is smart. Anyone who boasts about skipping tests is probably either just ignorant or they're coping for their own lack of skill.
